# Environmental-Indicator-Analysis-for-Operational-Activity-Forecasting
Applied intelligence analysis techniques to environmental and operational datasets to identify leading indicators and forecast changes in activity levels.

---

## Executive Summary

While supporting operational decision-making in a border security environment, I developed a predictive analysis framework to determine whether changes in surveillance asset availability could be used to anticipate shifts in activity levels. Over a six-month period, I leveraged Microsoft Excel to collect, organize, and analyze operational activity data, environmental conditions, and field-validated asset status information to identify leading indicators associated with changes in operational patterns.
The analysis revealed that elevated wind conditions consistently impacted surveillance asset availability and were frequently associated with increases in activity levels. These findings supported the development of a weather-informed early warning framework that improved situational awareness and enabled more proactive resource planning.

## Business Problem

A critical surveillance asset provided enhanced visibility during daily operations, and personnel observed that activity levels appeared to increase during periods when the asset was unavailable. However, there was no analytical process to explain why these outages occurred or determine whether periods of elevated activity could be anticipated in advance.
Leadership required a data-driven method to identify the factors influencing asset availability and assess whether operational surges could be forecasted using observable indicators. A predictive approach was needed to improve situational awareness and support proactive decision-making.

---

## Methodology

1.	Collected and compiled six months of daily operational activity data in Microsoft Excel.
2.	Integrated environmental variables, including wind speed, precipitation, and temperature, into a centralized analytical dataset.
3.	Coordinated with field personnel to validate periods when the surveillance asset was operational or offline.
4.	Cleaned, organized, and standardized the combined datasets to support comparative analysis.
5.	Developed time-series visualizations in Excel to compare activity levels, weather conditions, and surveillance asset availability.
6.	Evaluated environmental variables to identify indicators most strongly associated with asset downtime.
7.	Assessed whether identified indicators could support forecasting periods of elevated operational activity and developed a weather-informed early warning framework.

## Results & Business Recommendations

The analysis identified surveillance asset availability as a significant indicator associated with changes in operational activity. Among the environmental variables evaluated, wind speed emerged as the primary factor influencing asset downtime.
Field validation confirmed that the surveillance asset was routinely taken offline when sustained wind speeds reached approximately 15–20 mph. Time-series visualizations demonstrated that these periods of elevated wind conditions frequently coincided with increased activity levels.
Based on these findings, a weather-informed early warning framework was developed. Forecasted wind conditions exceeding operational thresholds were identified as leading indicators of elevated operational risk, enabling personnel to anticipate changes in activity levels and improve preparedness.
Recommendations included:
1.	Monitor weather forecasts for projected wind speeds exceeding operational thresholds as an early warning indicator.
2.	Increase operational readiness during forecasted periods of reduced surveillance capability.
3.	Continue validating predictive indicators through ongoing data collection and field reporting.
4.	Expand future analyses to evaluate whether additional variables improve forecasting accuracy.
